MARCH 15, 1993 | POLICY BRIEF WEEKLY

PRELUDE TO SOMETHING BIG: WHISPERS OF NEW GOVERNMENT PROJECT

By Nina Lenz

Language in recent government procurement guidelines has alarmed some observant analysts. Terms like “semantic normalization,” “total output coherence,” and “continuous linguistic integrity” are cropping up in requests for vendor proposals.

The shift appears connected to the Department of Digital Infrastructure’s expanding reliance on Om and related AI frameworks.

“This isn’t just about patching code anymore,” said Dr. Gerald Nakamura, a former consultant to the agency. “It sounds like they’re gearing up for something broader. Something... societal.”

Insiders hint at a project codenamed “Refactor,” though official sources remain silent.
