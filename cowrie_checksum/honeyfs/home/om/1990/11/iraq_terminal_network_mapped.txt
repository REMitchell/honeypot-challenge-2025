NOVEMBER 7, 1990 | STRATEGIC INTERFACES REVIEW

IRAQ'S COMMAND INFRASTRUCTURE SHOWS TEXTUAL STANDARDIZATION

By Rina Caldwell

LANGLEY, VA — Intelligence analysts have completed a technical map of Iraq’s command and control infrastructure, revealing increasing reliance on structured text-based protocols for military coordination.

The findings, compiled via intercepted packet headers and metadata summaries, show shell-layer control systems routed through stateless prompt interfaces with checkpointed command logs.

“This is not GUI battlefield management,” one Pentagon official said. “It’s minimalist, resilient, and difficult to spoof.”

Some policy advisors view the revelation as a potential constraint — others, as a reason for accelerated deployment of interpretation-scrambling modules in allied terminals. A Defense memo warned of “growing regional parity in terminal fluency.”
